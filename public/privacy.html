Privacy Policy

BigBricey · Last updated July 22, 2026

BigBricey is an invite-only adult beta. It is designed so you can use a nickname or pseudonym while keeping long-term fitness, nutrition, and wellness records private to your account.

What we collect

  • Sign-in: your verified Google email and provider account identifier. These are used for access and account recovery; BigBricey does not require a legal name in your health profile.
  • Health & wellness: food logs, workouts, steps, weight, goals, height, age/sex for targets, freeform notes you enter
  • Conversations: messages you send to the companion and its replies, so you can reopen chat history and retain recent context
  • Permanent memory: facts and preferences you explicitly ask BigBricey to remember, plus where the memory came from so you can review, edit, or delete it
  • Health Snapshots: private drafts you choose to save, including your edits
  • Product feedback you explicitly confirm. Conversation context is included only when you separately choose that option.
  • Content-free product events such as whether a log completed, response time, correction/clarification counts, and Snapshot use. These events do not contain message or food text.
  • Optional marketing preference (if you check it during setup)

How we use it

  • Power your personal log and daily dashboard
  • Store your forever ledger so history isn’t lost
  • Estimate calorie/macro targets from the stats you provide
  • Generate user-controlled summaries and structured exports from your own records
  • Operate and improve reliability, security, and the founding beta
  • If you opt in, occasional product updates (you can opt out)

What we don’t do

  • We don’t sell your health data
  • We don’t use third-party advertising trackers
  • Other members cannot see your food log or body metrics
  • We don’t automatically send Health Snapshots to doctors, other users, or outside AI tools
  • Feedback does not include conversation context unless you explicitly opt in when sending it

Storage & security

BigBricey uses random internal account identifiers, signed sessions, server-only service credentials, account-scoped database queries, rate limits, and access/mutation auditing. Data travels over HTTPS. The hosting and database providers report encryption for stored customer data. No Internet service can promise perfect security, and the beta is still subject to ongoing review and testing.

Service providers process data only for product functions: Google for sign-in, Vercel for hosting, Supabase for the private database, OpenRouter and the selected model provider for AI requests, and USDA/Open Food Facts for food lookups. Chat text needed for an AI answer is sent to the configured AI service. A food photo is sent only when you choose photo analysis; the image is used to create a review draft and is not added to your ledger or account history.

When voice dictation is available, your browser or device vendor may process microphone audio to produce text. BigBricey receives only the transcript you choose to send.

Your choices

You can change your nickname, companion settings, onboarding answers, memories, and logged records in the app. In You → Your data & privacy, you can record a full-export request or prepare a deletion request. Deletion is not automatic: the request is verified and reviewed to avoid erasing the wrong account.

Retention

Account and health records are retained while your beta account is active so long-term trends remain available. A verified deletion request starts the controlled deletion process. Limited security, audit, legal, and provider-backup records may be retained where reasonably necessary. A final written retention schedule and incident-response process are launch gates before a larger commercial beta.

Age

BigBricey currently accepts adults age 18 and older only. It is not marketed to children.

Contact

Questions or privacy requests: use the controls inside your signed-in account or contact the operator who invited you. A dedicated public privacy contact is required before wider enrollment.
